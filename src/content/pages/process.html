What to Expect on your First Visit with us

From pain relief to long-term wellness, explore answers to your questions about the conditions we treat and the care we provide.

1

Consultation

Your first visit begins with a thorough consultation to understand your specific concerns and health goals. Dr. Matthew Cohen takes the time to listen to your symptoms, medical history, and lifestyle factors that may be contributing to your condition. This step allows us to get a clear picture of your needs and ensures that your care plan is tailored to address the root causes of your discomfort.

2

Evaluation

Next, Dr. Cohen performs a detailed evaluation to assess your overall physical health and pinpoint areas of concern. This comprehensive assessment includes analyzing posture, range of motion, and muscle strength, as well as incorporating orthopedic tests, palpation, and reflex testing. By understanding how your body functions as a whole, we can identify potential issues affecting your spine and nervous system.

3

Necessary Testing

If needed, additional diagnostic testing such as X-rays or imaging studies may be performed to confirm the diagnosis and provide a deeper understanding of your condition. These tests help ensure that all aspects of your health are considered, allowing us to provide precise and effective care.

4

Treatment

Once your evaluation and testing are complete, Dr. Cohen begins your personalized treatment plan. This may include spinal adjustments, therapeutic exercises, traction therapy, or other techniques designed to alleviate pain and promote healing. From your very first treatment, the goal is to reduce discomfort, improve mobility, and set you on the path to long-term wellness.

Treatment Approaches You Can Expect

Dr.Cohen uses a variety of techniques to relieve pain, restore mobility, and enhance your overall well-being.   • Spinal Adjustments

    Spinal adjustments are the cornerstone of chiropractic care. By applying controlled force to specific joints in the spine, chiropractors restore proper alignment, reduce nerve irritation, and improve mobility. This non-invasive technique provides relief from pain caused by misalignments or tension.

  • Therapeutic Modalities

    Chiropractors often use therapeutic modalities such as electric muscle stimulation (EMS), ultrasound, and heat or cold therapy. These techniques enhance circulation, reduce inflammation, relieve muscle tension, and promote healing in soft tissues, providing an added layer of pain management.

  • Traction Therapy

    Traction therapy gently stretches the spine to relieve pressure on discs and nerves. This treatment is especially effective for conditions like herniated discs, spinal stenosis, or pinched nerves. It helps create space for healing and alleviates symptoms like pain, numbness, and tingling.

  • Therapeutic Exercises

    Chiropractors design personalized therapeutic exercise programs to strengthen supporting muscles, improve flexibility, and stabilize the spine. These exercises complement in-office treatments, reduce the risk of future injuries, and support long-term spinal health.

  • Posture and Ergonomic Coaching

    Poor posture and improper ergonomics often contribute to spinal issues. Chiropractors provide guidance on improving posture and recommend ergonomic adjustments for daily activities, such as sitting at a desk or lifting objects. These changes help prevent recurring pain and promote overall wellness
